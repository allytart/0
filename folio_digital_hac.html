A Homewood Arts Certificate Senior Design Exhibition

April - May 2016




Introduction

As students, the amount of time we spend burying ourselves in books and pixels seems insurmountable, yet the amount of time we do so in spaces which tame our work antics, is quite the opposite. Each space has its own personality, and the spaces we absorb ourselves in ultimately—however explicitly or subliminally—have an impact on our mental and physical state. Understanding these spaces will allow us to more effectively select environments to complement or combat our academic plights (think: an emotional surplus, weaning motivational charge, or the zipping jitters). Perhaps the next time you find yourself scurrying to the coffee shop on a dreary day, it has to do with more than just coffee. Be mindful of your mental posture, your goals, and your space. Which spaces do you know?

Purpose

This visual series is an artistic representation of the characteristics associated with each of these spaces, as we know them, with emphasis on emotion, flow, and initial impressions. This installation is made in place to illuminate the varied personalities of spaces across campus, and encourage more thoughtful utility of the spaces we have, for most appropriate tasks at hand.
