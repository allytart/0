2016 Main Symposium

Main Event, March 2016

*Instructions Not Included



Overview

This year, we've gathered 7 speakers who have seen a significant part of their life unveil thematically as "*Instructions Not Included." This TEDxJHU symposium seeks to expound the notion of unconventionality, not as an end goal, but as a byproduct of living willfully and engaging intelligently.

Role

As the design director, I led the full artistic production (theme ideation, print flyer and web graphics, buttons, geofilters, stickers, livestream graphics, set design) for the event.

Salon, 2016

What They Didn't Tell You At Orientation



Overview

Our first salon event of the year attracted an attendance which tripled our expected capacity, featured two student speakers, and provided a full hot-chocolate bar (thanks to Bon Appetit and Spoon U!) with additional entertainment from the All-Nighters acapella group and the fire-spinning Entertainer's Club.

Role

As the design director, I led the full artistic production (visual concept ideation, graphics, buttons, stickers, set design) for the event. Our set design was constricted by the tight stage space of Bamboo Cafe, but optimized through vertical space.

Main Event, 2015

NextGen:TBD



Overview

NextGen:TBD was the theme for TEDx JohnsHopkinsUniversity's main event in March 2015. These banners were designed for the official launch of the event and to bring exposure to the theme, which with the taglineTBD, is "meant to underscore the notion that those at the conference could play a part in determining what the future will hold."


The NextGen Project was created to give student groups on campus a chance to be part of the main event in March. Because our theme is an invitation to all in contribution of the next generation, we wanted to extend the opportunity to our own Johns Hopkins students.

Graphics

The two primary event banners were used interchangeably, with one to promote the event and the other to generate a distinguished visual presence of TEDx JohnsHopkinsUniversity on the college campuses as well as on the web. The third banner (not pictured) was made in part to encourage participation through our live Twitter feed at the main event, where people could interact with the speakers through question submissions. The banner is QR-scan compatible, and can be used for our social media publicity at any time. Brochures, set design, stickers, buttons, and all promotional material were designed and assembled by the design and marketing team.

Stage Design

For our NextGen:TBD theme, we had hundreds of empty printouts resembling polaroids, categorized as "then," "now," and "NextGen:TBD," which contained names and drawings of categorically relevant milestones, written by both the TEDxJHU team and audience members.
