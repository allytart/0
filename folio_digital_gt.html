Gilman Tunnel Interactive Projection System

JOHNS HOPKINS DIGITAL MEDIA CENTER INTERACTIVE ARTS GRANT PROJECT

An Electrical Computer Engineering Design Team Initiative

September 2015 - May 2016

Project Brief

Background

Before construction at the Gilman building, the Gilman Tunnels used to be home to a stretch of mural boards where student groups on campus would paint over to advertise various events around campus. Since construction, however, the mural boards have been neglected and overtaken by a plethora of flyers and posters. The Gilman Tunnels, to this day, still remain largely underused, only serving primarily as a passageway.

Objective

Transform a neglected, but prime (an ideal shield against the elements), passageway on the Johns Hopkins University Homewood campus into an enjoyable and stress-reducing space. This project had received the annual Digital Media Center Creative Use of Technology Grant, which supported the project's launch last year, with a functional interactive display experience. The team this year is building upon the idea of an interactive light display, with a focused project development design to bring a "Lava Hop(kins)-scotch" to the Gilman Tunnels using a projection system and Microsoft Kinect technology. The projected images will be responsive to the movements of the individual through the environment, and create an interactive and fun experience for passer-by's who choose to interact with the system.

Responsibilities

  • In this team I was the sole designer, and led the visual direction, as well as graphics, animations, and style developments for the project. Graphics and animations were created using Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, and Adobe AfterEffects, and coded in Processing.

01

Design Proposal & Concept Finalization + Timeline

The purpose of the project was evaluated, and a game of "Lava Hopscotch" was chosen in order to bring an interactive, nostalgic, and light-hearted projection game to the Gilman Tunnel.

02

Design Elements, Game Logic, and Coding

During this stage, we developed the game logic and started on visual design. I experimented with various game aesthetics (colors, styles, themes) in creating a final set of game screens. After example game screens were designed, the team started converting the elements of the design to the game arena in code.

03

Iterative Testing

The visual aesthetics, game logic, and animations were all a process that went through numeous iterations. The team went through multiple stages of design, artistic themes, and styles.

04

Final Presentation: ECE Design Day

Final poster exhibit and projection game demonstration.
