Perceived Cognition & Tools for Synthesis

INTERFACES DESIGNED TO MINIMIZE ILLUSIONS IN LEARNING

Jan 2015 - May 2016

Background

Objective

Many people are unaware of the holes in their learning, which may be accumulated over time. Our focus is to research and analyze frequent roadblocks confronted (or lack thereof: illusions) in the process of learning and retention, and design interfaces which provide tools, techniques, and structure to help probe and engage relevant cognition for better understanding.

Goals

  • Develop an interface to aid in learning, incorporating strategies of inquiry-based learning, synthesizing, and spaced-retrieval prompting, in targeting the retention and storage of information from short-term to long-term memory
  • Devise pertinent concept-testing research experiment which aims to study how students interact with various material, identify and reason through questions, and adopt strategies for learning

Foundational Research:

  • Learning strategies
  • Counseling techniques
  • Inquiry-based learning
  • Psychology research

Iterative Wireframing & Prototyping

  • If the architecture of the resource resembles something that exits (i.e. grids resembling excel spreadsheets), then the rules/restraints from those objects will be carried over (from previous example: "can I draw?")
  • Strategies are only helpful if pertinent to the material, so more options would provide for more appropriate and fitting use (ex: article about Northern Lights proved difficult to use with the strategy of creating analogies)
  • We found that in studying any material, the process is often as follows:
    1. Categorizing (low-level analysis, sorting)
    2. Similarities (synthesizing)
    3. Rephrasing (testing self)

From these findings, we simplified the features for the app accordingly, and created redesigns for the user interface.
